# LLVM Qualification Group’s March 2026 Sync-Up Agenda

The next sync-up meeting will be a dedicated joint session with members of the Security Response WG :raising\_hands: Key subjects are:

- **Testing & Traceability**
  Exchange on current challenges around identifying, structuring, and deriving evidence from security- and safety-relevant tests across LLVM components. Discussion may include test annotation ideas to support traceability (i.e. function :left\_right\_arrow: test).
- **Threat Model & Points of Interaction Between Safety/Security Standards**
  Conversation around the Security Response WG’s trust-based threat model RFC and how it may intersect with functional safety and cybersecurity expectations (e.g., ISO 26262, ISO 21434), especially from a toolchain integrator’s perspective.
- **Collaboration Paths & Possible Pilots**
  Identify concrete next steps, potential pilot components, or areas where continued coordination between the Security Response & Qualification WGs would be beneficial.

To help make the discussion concrete and productive, participants are encouraged to briefly review the following resources beforehand:

- Security Response WG’s trust-based threat model proposal:
  <https://github.com/mrragava/llvm-project/blob/ragava/threat-model-proposal/llvm/docs/ThreatModel.md>
- [@petbernt](https://discourse.llvm.org/u/petbernt)’s Function-based Qualification of C/C++ Standard Library Functions proposal:
  <https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1-JpF6kMbth1hL0ZkwRyHQ9A7edqd2vwM3U7Y9QcfQfI/edit?slide=id.g3acb06ff9f7_2_258#slide=id.g3acb06ff9f7_2_258>
- (Shared by [@YoungJunLee](https://discourse.llvm.org/u/youngjunlee))
  Paper on integrated safety & security co-analysis under ISO 26262 and ISO/SAE 21434:
  <https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/24/6/1848>

If possible, we would appreciate if you could come with concrete thoughts, questions, examples, or ideas on where you see potential alignment (or tension) between these efforts (e.g., small sketches of how test annotation or traceability could work in practice for both domains).

Looking forward to the discussion. See you at the sync-up!
